History

In early 2019, this project started from an idea of creating a virtual 'world', where not only terrain but also live weather, climate zone, accurate flora, fauna and timezone would be rendered based on a physical location.

1. Accurate timezone 2. Live weather 3. Flora and fauna accurate to climate zone and season 4. Terrain from a DEM dataset (rivers, lakes and oceans also to be rendered, other terrain details may be considered)
* DEM: Digital Elevation Model

This idea, and the ideas that follow, were all thought of with the same goal in mind: to capture, at least partly, the beauty of our Earth. I was most inspired by satellite images that I saw on zoom.earth. Specifically, I found the satellite image of the northern peninsula of Tazovsky District, Russia to be particularly captivating. The landscape, with strangely circular lakes and mountains that make the whole peninsula appear like crumbling paint, wasn't anything that I would expect from a satellite imagery; it appeared almost unreal and alien. I'm certain that, if anyone spends a bit of time zooming into these uninhabited regions that only appear as monochromatic shapes in most maps, they will find beauty that is unique.

The perspective that lead to the creation of Landscape v.1 was that one will be able to experience this beauty if they were literally on such landscapes. The terrain is rendered in grey, and I have spent a significant amount of time rendering accurate constellations, as a means of adding reality to the scene. It is certainly a step toward my 'goal', but it is also true that anywhere on the Earth tend to look similar, if not identical, with this. I think it leads to an important lesson that the 'beauty' of the Earth, with all its uniqueness, is very complex; simply copying some elements of the real world without a proper and personal understanding in what exactly causes that beauty fails to convey a meaning.
